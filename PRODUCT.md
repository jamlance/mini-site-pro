# Mini-site Pro — Product Context

register: brand

## What it is
An embedded Inkress app that lets a small Jamaican merchant build a one-page storefront ("mini-site") in minutes: block-based builder, products, theme, custom handle/domain, real Inkress hosted checkout. Two surfaces with different jobs:
- **The storefront** (`/s/:handle`, custom domains) — what the merchant's *customers* see. Design IS the product here. It must look like a storefront the owner is proud to share on WhatsApp/Instagram. **Brand register.**
- **The editor** (builder/products/theme/overview, embedded in the Inkress dashboard) — the tool the merchant uses. Calm, fast, confidence-inspiring. **Product register** (secondary surface; the storefront leads).

## Users
Owner-operators of local JM businesses: food shops, salons/barbers, makers, small retail. Non-technical. They judge the storefront the way a customer would, on their phone. They want it to look "real," warm, and trustworthy, not like a generic template.

## Brand / tone
Warm, local, honest, hand-made-feeling, premium-but-approachable. The shared Inkress consumer language: warm OKLCH neutrals (paper, clay), Bricolage Grotesque display + Hanken Grotesk body, tabular-num prices, generous editorial spacing. Confident, not corporate. Jamaican small-business pride, not Silicon-Valley SaaS.

## Anti-references (what to never look like)
- Generic SaaS template: system-ui, pure `#fff`/`#000`, identical icon-heading-text card grids, centered hero with a stock photo and a pill button. (This is exactly what the v1 looked like and was rejected.)
- Cheap `linear-gradient(135deg, …)` hero banners.
- Square Online / Linktree blandness.

## Strategic principles
1. The storefront must not be guessable as "a website builder template." Real type, real rhythm, characterful blocks.
2. The merchant's chosen accent is the brand color; the chrome (paper/ink/spacing/type) is the premium constant underneath.
3. Every block earns a distinct layout. No eight-blocks-all-look-like-the-same-card.
4. Mobile-first: most views are a phone.
